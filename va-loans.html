Who Qualifies for a VA Loan in Oregon?

VA loans are available to a broad range of service members, veterans, and their families. I can confirm your specific status and pull your Certificate of Eligibility directly:

  • ✓
    Veterans: 90 consecutive days of active service during wartime, or 181 days during peacetime
  • ✓
    Active Duty: Currently serving with 90+ days of continuous active service
  • ✓
    National Guard / Reserves: 6 years of service, or 90 days active under Title 10 or Title 32 orders
  • ✓
    Surviving Spouses: Un-remarried spouses of veterans who died in service or from a service-connected disability
  • ✓
    Credit: No VA minimum; most lenders prefer 580+; manual underwriting available
  • ✓
    Property: Must be a primary residence and meet VA Minimum Property Requirements (MPR)

VA Loan FAQ — Oregon

Do VA loans require a down payment in Oregon?
No. Eligible veterans with full VA entitlement can purchase a home anywhere in Oregon with $0 down. There is no minimum down payment requirement for borrowers with full entitlement.
Are there VA loan limits in Oregon?
Since 2020, VA loan limits were eliminated for borrowers with full entitlement. You can borrow what your income and credit support without a county-based cap.
Can I combine a VA loan with a rate buydown?
Yes. VA loans are fully compatible with rate buydowns — including 2-1 buydowns, 1-0 buydowns, and permanent rate reductions. The seller can even contribute to the buydown cost. This combination can significantly reduce your monthly payment.
What credit score do I need for a VA loan?
The VA itself sets no minimum credit score. Most lenders prefer 580+. I specialize in manual underwriting for veterans with complex credit histories.
What is the VA Funding Fee?
The VA Funding Fee is a one-time fee that ranges from 1.25% to 3.3% depending on your down payment and whether it's your first VA loan. Veterans with a service-connected disability rating are exempt entirely.
